# BJP's 2026 performance walkthrough

A guided tour through BJP's 2026 results, starting from the 3 wins and expanding outward through cohorts. Each section presents data, names patterns visible in the data, and stops there. No reverse-engineering of strategy, no 2031 forecasts.

---

## The 3 wins

BJP won three Assembly constituencies in 2026: **Nemom**, **Kazhakoottam**, **Chathannoor**. All three converged on four observable patterns visible in the data.

### Religion and caste mix — substantially more Hindu than the state average

The 3 wins (Nemom 69%, Kazhakoottam 69%, Chathannoor 72%) average **~70% Hindu vs the state's 53%** — about 17 percentage points above average. Muslim share is correspondingly lower (~14% vs 26% statewide); Christian share is roughly state-level (~16% vs 18%). On caste, the 3 wins sit in **Trivandrum district** (Nair 39%, Ezhava 27%) and **Kollam district** (Nair 28%, Ezhava 33% — Chathannoor only) — both Nair-meaningful districts above the state mean. *Caste data is district-level only, so this is approximate, not AC-precise.*

### Geographic cluster

All 3 wins are in or adjacent to Trivandrum district:

- **Nemom** (Trivandrum, AC 135)
- **Kazhakoottam** (Trivandrum, AC 132)
- **Chathannoor** (Kollam, AC 126 — northernmost Kollam, geographically adjacent to Trivandrum-district seats)

[map: Trivandrum + northern Kollam districts; 3 BJP wins highlighted]

### Mature vote-share base going in

| AC | 2026 BJP candidate | NDA 2021 base | NDA 2026 share | BJP Δ |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Nemom | Rajeev Chandrasekhar | 35.5% | 40.9% | +5.4pp |
| Kazhakoottam | V. Muraleedharan | 29.1% | 35.7% | +6.6pp |
| Chathannoor | B.B. Gopakumar | 30.6% | 38.2% | +7.6pp |

All 3 wins had NDA-2021 base ≥ 29% — already structurally near a winning share before the 2026 cycle. Across Kerala's 140 ACs, only 8 seats had NDA bases at this level going in.

### Anti-LDF swing benefit

In 2026, statewide alliance shifts (mean of per-AC vote shares across 140 ACs): **LDF −7.6pp, UDF +7.0pp, NDA +2.0pp**. The math balances cleanly: LDF lost ~7.6pp + Others lost ~1.5pp ≈ 9pp redistributed, of which ~7pp went to UDF and ~2pp went to NDA. The dominant pattern is "anti-LDF swing landed on UDF, with a meaningful slice landing on NDA."

But at 21 specific seats, the swing inverted — **NDA gained more than UDF**. The 3 wins are all in this set. See [Wave-capture](#wave-capture) for the full list.

## Low-base breakouts <a id="low-base-breakouts"></a>

*2021 effective NDA baseline < 10% **AND** BJP Δ ≥ +10pp in 2026. **3 seats qualify** — trimmed from a wider earlier list.*

### Why the trim

The earlier version of this group included 6 seats. Three (Guruvayoor, Thalassery, Devikulam) had **zero NDA presence in 2021** — neither BJP nor BDJS nor Twenty 20 fielded. Those are re-entry from non-fielding, not "new growth from a low base." Without a 2021 NDA-equivalent baseline to compare against, we can't reliably attribute 2026 share as growth.

| AC | Seat | District | H/C/M | 2021 base | 2026 BJP | Δ | Outcome | Mechanism |
|---|---|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---|---|
| 93 | Pala | Kottayam | 38/52/10 | 7.9% (BJP) | 26.1% (BJP) | +18.2 | UDF | Marquee Christian (Shone George) at Christian-majority seat |
| 116 | Karunagappally | Kollam | 65/5/28 | 7.0% (BJP) | 18.7% (BJP) | +11.6 | UDF | Same-vehicle continuity — BJP fielded both years, organic build |
| 127 | Varkala | Trivandrum | 71/4/25 | 8.3% (BDJS) | 19.9% (BJP) | +11.6 | LDF | Slot rotation — BDJS held in 2021, BJP took back in 2026 |

Three different mechanisms within "new growth from low base": marquee Christian candidate at a Christian-majority seat (Pala); organic build from a real BJP base (Karunagappally); alliance-vehicle slot rotation (Varkala) — the +11.6pp Δ here is partly the alliance-vehicle effect, not pure new-voter movement.

## Vote-share decomposition

How NDA's three constituent parties (BJP, BDJS, Twenty 20) added up to the alliance total in 2021 vs 2026, and what the flat statewide aggregate hides about *where* BJP actually moved.

### TL;DR — three lenses, three different stories

> All of BJP's net 2026 growth happened inside ~21% of Kerala's seats. The flat statewide aggregate is the average of three very different regimes: a targeted 30-seat set where BJP grew strongly, a neutral middle, and a negative-space set where BJP declined or sat out.

| Lens | n | BJP Δ |
|---|---:|---:|
| Statewide (all 140 ACs, mean per-AC) | 140 | **+0.30pp** |
| Where BJP fielded *both* years (paired) | 89 | **+1.65pp** |
| Targeted seats (cohort union) | 30 | **+5.16pp** |

Same data, three lenses. The statewide figure includes ACs where BJP didn't field (counts as 0%), dragging the mean toward zero. The paired view at 89 ACs strips that out. The targeted view zooms further to the 30 ACs where BJP put real effort.

### NDA party decomposition (statewide)

| Party | 2021 | 2026 | Δ |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| BJP (Bharatiya Janata Party) | 11.29% | 11.58% | +0.30pp |
| BDJS (Bharath Dharma Jana Sena) | 1.14% | 1.42% | +0.28pp |
| T20 (Twenty 20 Party) | 0.75% | 1.51% | +0.76pp |

Three things visible:

1. **BJP was essentially flat at the aggregate** (+0.30pp on a base of 11.29%) — within rounding of "no change."

### Footprint and intensity

A different angle: count only the ACs where the party fielded a candidate that year.

| Party | 2021 fielded | 2021 share-where-fielded | 2026 fielded | 2026 share-where-fielded |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| BJP | 115 ACs | 13.74% | 98 ACs | 16.50% |
| BDJS | 21 ACs | 7.61% | 22 ACs | 9.04% |
| T20 | 8 ACs | 13.14% | 19 ACs | 11.18% |

- **BJP shrank footprint** (115 → 98 candidates, −15%). At the same time, the average share at the seats where it did field rose from 13.74% to 16.50%. **Concentration trade**: less ground, more depth.
- **T20's footprint more than doubled** (8 → 19 ACs). Average per-fielded share dropped because the new ACs are thinner terrain, but aggregate impact still grew (+0.76pp statewide).
- **BDJS held footprint roughly steady** (21 → 22) with a small per-fielded uptick.

This is a different lens from the paired comparison above. The paired view (89 ACs BJP fielded *both* years) gave +1.65pp — "growth at unchanged contest sites." The footprint view above shows BJP also shifted *which* sites it contested. Both effects compound.

### What this changes about the BJP performance story

The standard "BJP roughly flat statewide" framing is technically correct but misleading about *what BJP was trying to do*. Same data, three lenses, three answers:

| Question | Lens | BJP Δ |
|---|---|---:|
| Did BJP become a bigger statewide party? | Statewide 140-AC mean | +0.30pp |
| Where it competed in both cycles, did it grow? | Paired (89 ACs) | +1.65pp |
| Where it targeted, did it move the needle? | Targeted (30 ACs) | **+5.16pp** |

The targeted set captures effectively all of BJP's net growth. The remaining 110 ACs are either neutral (slight decline) or negative-space (clear decline).

## Methodology

**Effective baselines.** Comparing 2016/2021/2026 BJP-only shares produces misleading deltas when BJP didn't field but BDJS/Twenty 20 did, or when the same individual ran under a different party. We compute an effective NDA baseline at each AC for each cycle in priority order: BJP share if BJP fielded; same-individual share under any party (e.g. P.C. George at Poonjar — Independent 2016, KJS 2021, BJP 2026); BDJS share; Twenty 20 share; 0% otherwise. Candidate continuity is resolved via `data/candidate-aliases.json`.

**2021 alliance attribution.** JD(S), LJD, NCP small-ally allocations to LDF in 2021 are credited to LDF (not OTHER) via per-candidate `alliance` attribution in `data/historical/`.

**Vote-share methodologies.** Three different views appear in this card. Each answers a different question.

| Method | What it includes | Question it answers |
|---|---|---|
| (a) Mean across all 140 ACs | Every AC, with 0% where BJP didn't field | "Average share across the state" — uniform-effort assumption |
| (b) Paired across ACs fielded both years | The 89 ACs where BJP contested in 2021 *and* 2026 | "Where they competed both times, did they grow?" |
| (c) Mean across each year's fielded set | 115 ACs in 2021; 98 in 2026 (different sets) | "Strength at each year's contest set" — footprint-aware |

The three-lens TL;DR uses (a) for statewide and (b) for the paired view. The Footprint section uses (c). A fourth check — **total-vote aggregate** (sum-of-votes statewide ÷ all valid votes) — gives BJP 11.35% → 11.49% (+0.13pp) and NDA 12.51% → 14.28% (+1.77pp). Per-AC mean and total-vote aggregate give the same qualitative story; differences are within ~0.3pp.

**Computation details.** NOTA excluded from 2026 denominator; 2021 NOTA included (matches Python upstream-script asymmetry, see `src/lib/data/narrative-metrics.ts:102` for rationale). All party-name canonicalization via `data/alliances.json`.

**Targeted-cohort union**: mature growers ∪ low-base breakouts ∪ declining mature ∪ wave-capture. 30 unique seats. Overlaps: mature growers ∩ wave-capture 7 seats, low-base breakouts ∩ wave-capture 3, declining mature ∩ wave-capture 1 (Poonjar).
