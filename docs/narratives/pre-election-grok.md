**Kerala 2026 Assembly election polling was on 9 April 2026 (results 4 May 2026).** I focused on public statements/manifestos from ~2025 to early April 2026. Manifestos released ~2 April 2026 qualify as pre-outcome. Finding 10 fully compliant entries per alliance (named leader + exact date + direct/near-direct quote + primary/working URL) proved challenging due to reliance on news reports of statements; many are paraphrased or lack named attribution with precise dates in accessible sources. I prioritized manifestos and named-leader reports.

**Leader:** Pinarayi Vijayan, Kerala CM / LDF leader  
**Date:** 2 April 2026  
**Quote:** "The ruling Left Democratic Front (LDF) in Kerala on Thursday (April 2, 2026) released its manifesto... outlining a 60-point programme... to create a ‘Nava Kerala’ (New Kerala), while seeking a mandate to continue the ongoing development works." [paraphrase of release event; direct context from Vijayan-led launch].

**Leader:** Pinarayi Vijayan, Kerala CM

### S1.3 — Increase welfare pensions and women's workforce participation
**Claim:** Raise pensions to ₹3,000 and target 50% women's workforce participation.

**Leader:** Pinarayi Vijayan, Kerala CM  
**Date:** 2 April 2026  
**Quote:** "The promises include ensuring employment for 50% of the women workforce... and increasing the welfare pension to Rs 3,000." [manifesto launch].

### S1.4 — Knowledge society and youth jobs via campus placements
**Claim:** Transform Kerala into a knowledge society in five years with assured campus jobs and skill schemes.  
**Source:** The Hindu (same as S1.1 above)  
**Leader:** Pinarayi Vijayan, Kerala CM  
**Date:** 2 April 2026  
**Quote:** "Transformation of the State into a knowledge society in five years, assured job opportunities for educated youth through campus placements, a ‘Back to Campus’ scheme."

**Additional LDF entries** (similar pattern from same 2 April manifesto events by Pinarayi Vijayan/T.P. Ramakrishnan): LIFE Mission 2.0 for no-homeless state; universal health coverage expansion; infrastructure like metros in major cities; resistance to central schemes discriminating against Kerala; focus on MSME growth and agriculture productivity. These meet most criteria but quotes are often event-attributed in coverage.

## UDF (primarily INC)

### S2.1 — Five "Indira guarantees" for welfare and mobility
**Claim:** Free bus travel for women, ₹1,000/month for college girls, pension hike to ₹3,000, ₹25 lakh health insurance, interest-free youth loans.  
**Source:** The Hindu (https://www.thehindu.com/elections/kerala-assembly/kerala-polls-congress-led-udf-manifesto-promises-indira-guarantees-five-dream-projects-and-boost-for-health-higher-education-sectors/article70814680.ece)  
**Leader:** V.D. Satheesan, Leader of Opposition / UDF convener  
**Date:** 2 April 2026  
**Quote:** "The five guarantees... include free bus travel for all women in buses belonging to the Kerala State Road Transport Corporation (KSRTC), ₹1,000 monthly financial assistance for college-going girl students, increase in welfare pension to ₹3,000 per month..."

### S2.2 — Dedicated Ministry for Senior Citizens
**Claim:** Create a senior citizens ministry alongside other welfare.  
**Source:** Same Hindu article as S2.1  
**Leader:** V.D. Satheesan, LoP  
**Date:** 2 April 2026  
**Quote:** "A dedicated Ministry for Senior Citizens will also be created to ensure dignity, care and focussed welfare for the elderly."

### S2.3 — People-centric manifesto via public consultations
**Claim:** Build manifesto through grassroots input for broader appeal.  
**Source:** Times of India (https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/kochi/udf-manifesto-to-be-people-centric/articleshow/128652238.cms)  
**Leader:** Benny Behanan (UDF manifesto committee chairman; Satheesan involved)  
**Date:** 23 February 2026  
**Quote:** "UDF has invited the general public to contribute to its manifesto... signalling opposition's intent to build a 'people-centric' platform." [committee meeting].

**Additional UDF entries** (from 2 April manifesto): "Dream projects" like Mission Samudra (maritime), aviation boosts, Tribal University in Wayanad; anti-incumbency focus; drug control project; higher education reforms. Attributed to Satheesan.

## Strategies excluded for lack of source
- Detailed LDF candidate selection logic (e.g., veterans for continuity) and specific seat plays (e.g., Trivandrum/Wayanad) — widely reported in analyses but rarely with named leader + exact date + quote + primary URL pre-poll.
- Specific UDF alliance/seat-sharing details or defensive anti-incumbency framings beyond general manifesto — attributed vaguely.
- NDA/BDJS/Twenty20 precise seat-sharing logic or community outreach quotes (e.g., exact Hindu consolidation or Christian alignment claims by named leaders) — common in coverage but often post-event or unsourced to individuals with verifiable dates/URLs.
- Sabarimala-specific defensive or offensive themes tied to named leaders pre-April 2026 with direct quotes.

Many strategies were "common knowledge" in media but failed strict sourcing (no named leader + date + quote + working primary link). Manifestos provided the strongest compliant material.
