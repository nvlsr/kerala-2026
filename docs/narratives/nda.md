## TL;DR

- **BJP 2026: targeted consolidation, not statewide expansion.** Statewide BJP aggregate +0.30pp (essentially flat on an 11.29% base); inside a 30-seat targeted set BJP grew **+5.16pp**.
- **3 wins**: Nemom, Kazhakoottam, Chathannoor — all in Trivandrum + adjacent Kollam. All three share four features: substantially Hindu-heavier than state (~70% vs 53%), Trivandrum geographic cluster, mature NDA base ≥29% going in, captured anti-LDF wave away from UDF.
- **6 cohorts decompose BJP's footprint:**
  - **Mature growers** (n=11) — meaningful growth on existing base. All 3 wins here.
  - **Low-base breakouts** (n=3) — Pala, Karunagappally, Varkala. Heterogeneous mechanisms (marquee candidate / organic build / slot rotation).
  - **Declining mature** (n=8) — positive 2021 base, ≥−2pp Δ. 6 of 8 in Christian-significant terrain.
  - **Wave-capture** (n=21) — NDA Δ > UDF Δ, NDA share ≥15%, LDF Δ < 0. All 3 wins here.
  - **Strategic abstention** (n=14) — BJP fielded zero across 3 cycles. BDJS/Twenty 20 hold slots.
  - **Structural exclusion** (n=19) — BJP+BDJS aggregate <8% across 3 cycles. 9 of 19 in Malappuram.
- **Footprint trade**: BJP shrank candidacies (115 → 98 ACs) while average share where fielded rose (13.74% → 16.50%). Less ground, more depth.
- **NDA party decomposition**: BJP +0.30, BDJS +0.28, Twenty 20 +0.76 (+100% on its 0.75% base — formally inducted into NDA January 2026, contested 19 ACs vs 8 in 2021).
- **Of NDA's 14.28% statewide vote share, only 5% converted to seat-winning effort.** 95% of NDA votes wasted. Highest of any alliance.

Four observable patterns shared:

1. **Religion**: Hindu share ~70% vs state's 53% — ~17pp above average. Muslim share lower (~14% vs 26%). Christian share ~16% (roughly state-level).
2. **Caste (district-level)**: Trivandrum district (Nair 39%, Ezhava 27%) and Kollam district (Nair 28%, Ezhava 33% — Chathannoor only). Both Nair-meaningful districts above state mean.
3. **Geographic cluster**: Trivandrum district + northernmost Kollam district (Chathannoor geographically adjacent to TVM seats).
4. **Mature base**: NDA-2021 base ≥29% in all 3 — already structurally near a winning share before 2026. Only 8 Kerala ACs had NDA bases at this level going in.

Kazhakoottam was decided by 428 votes — narrowest of the 3 wins; statistically near-noise.

## §10 — Mechanism, what's open, what would weaken

### What's directly observable

- 3 BJP wins concentrated in Trivandrum + adjacent Kollam (Hindu-heavy, mature-base, wave-capture-aligned).
- BJP statewide flat aggregate hides +5.16pp gain in 30-seat targeted set.
- BJP shrank footprint 115 → 98, lifted per-fielded share 13.74 → 16.50%.
- Twenty 20 induction is the most aggregive party-level move: contested 19 ACs (vs 8), +0.76pp statewide (a 100% increase on its base).
- 6 of 8 declining-mature seats are in Christian-significant terrain.
- 56% of Malappuram's 16 ACs are persistently structurally-excluded NDA territory.

### What's NOT proven

- **The 3 wins are durable.** All 3 are first-time BJP wins in those seats. Kazhakoottam (428 votes) is barely a win.
- **The concentration is strategic.** BJP's geography emerged from underlying religion-region patterns. Whether the cohort allocation was tactical or reactive isn't separable here.
- **Marquee Christian candidates are a generalisable BJP strategy.** Pala (+18) and Thiruvalla (+14) worked; Thrissur (Padmaja Venugopal, −8) didn't. Heterogeneous.
- **T20 voters are NDA voters.** T20 was a Kitex-Garments-backed local party until 2026. Its induction into NDA happened January 2026 — voter overlap with BJP's core base is untested.

### What would weaken the headline

- **2031 BJP retention of Nemom/Kazhakoottam/Chathannoor under different swing conditions** → confirms the 3 wins are structural, not anti-LDF-wave-specific.
- **2031 BJP expansion into the 30-AC targeted set** → confirms targeted consolidation strategy was a building block. Failure to expand would suggest 2026 was peak-targeted-consolidation given current organisational reach.
- **Multi-cycle data showing the BJP-Pala-marquee-Christian effect persists** → Pala +18pp was Shone George at a Christian-majority seat. If that strategy works at Christian-belt seats more broadly, it's a generalisable BJP move; if only at Pala, it's George-specific.

## Methodology notes

### Effective NDA baseline

Comparing 2016/2021/2026 BJP-only shares produces misleading deltas when BJP didn't field but BDJS/Twenty 20 did, or when the same individual ran under a different party. Effective NDA baseline at each AC for each cycle uses priority order:
1. BJP share if BJP fielded
2. Same-individual share under any party (e.g. P.C. George at Poonjar — Indep 2016, KJS 2021, BJP 2026)
3. BDJS share
4. Twenty 20 share
5. 0% otherwise
