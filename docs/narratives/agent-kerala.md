Sources drawn primarily from Onmanorama (English Manorama), Mathrubhumi English, Madhyamam (English), The South First (Kerala desk), The News Minute, The Federal, Open The Magazine, The Week and ThePrint. Where Malayalam-only sources appeared (Madhyamam Malayalam, Kaumudi Malayalam) they were noted but not paraphrased. Coverage emphasises seat-level dynamics, community-specific shifts (Latin Catholic, Knanaya, Syro-Malabar, Samastha-aligned Sunnis, Ezhava, Nair, Pentecostal) and Kerala-specific issues (Munambam Waqf, Sabarimala gold, K-Rail, CMRL/Exalogic, rebel CPM revolts).

### 1. Munambam Waqf dispute drove Latin Catholic fishing belt away from LDF — and into a partial BJP embrace

**Claim:** Around 600 Latin Catholic fishing families in Munambam (Vypin/Paravur, Ernakulam) had been protesting the Kerala Waqf Board's claim on land they hold since the 1950s. After Parliament passed the Waqf Amendment Bill, residents burst crackers and chanted "Hail Modi" at 1 a.m. on April 3 (2025). Onmanorama and The South First reported KCBC (Kerala Catholic Bishops Council — covering Syro-Malabar, Syro-Malankara and Latin churches) actively lobbied MPs to support the amendment, while Suresh Gopi placed a St Mary statue at the protest site. UDF MP Hibi Eden warned residents the BJP was "trying to create conflict between Muslim and Christian communities." Resident Shyni Martin told The News Minute her family had "always been traditionally aligned with the Congress…but now we feel forced to" shift. The LDF's appointment of its Waqf Board nominee in Feb 2026 was framed by Onmanorama as keeping Munambam "simmering ahead of polls."

### 2. Sabarimala "lingering memory" plus the gold-misappropriation scandal hammered Devaswom Ministers and broke Hindu loyalty in Central Travancore

**Claim:** Onmanorama and Organiser reported that anger over the 2018 women-entry decision compounded with the 2026 Sabarimala gold scandal — alleged misappropriation of gold from the Dwarapalaka idols and Sreekovil door-frames probed by a High Court SIT — was decisive against LDF Devaswom and former Devaswom ministers. UDF candidate Nattakom Suresh in Ettumanoor turned the campaign chorus "Swarnam kattath aarappa" (Who stole the gold?) against Cooperation Minister V.N. Vasavan, and won by 19,752 votes. Former Devaswom minister Kadakampally Surendran lost Kazhakoottam to V. Muraleedharan (BJP) by 428 votes; Organiser singled out his and Vasavan's "folded-hands" posture obstructing devotee darshan as a recurring grievance. Health Minister Veena George lost Aranmula (a Sabarimala-route seat) to Abin Varkey Kodiyattu by 18,985 votes.

### 4. IUML's youth + women refresh in Malappuram blunted SDPI/Welfare Party challenge and produced Kerala's largest-ever winning margin

**Claim:** Onmanorama analysis credited Panakkad Sadiq Ali Shihab Thangal's "calibrated strategy" — IUML won 22 of 27 seats (its highest assembly tally), swept all seats in Malappuram and Kozhikode, and P.K. Kunhalikutty took Malappuram by 85,327 votes, the largest margin in Kerala assembly history. Fathima Thahiliya became IUML's first woman MLA, defeating CPM's T.P. Ramakrishnan in Perambra (an LDF-held seat) despite internal IUML dissent over her candidacy. The party also defeated former IUML defectors now in rival camps including K.T. Jaleel and P.T.A. Rahim. In Manjeshwaram, A.K.M. Ashraf defeated BJP state president K. Surendran by over 29,000 votes. The Welfare Party (Jamaat-e-Islami's arm) chose not to contest and indirectly supported UDF; SDPI's vote-split potential failed to materialise as Muslim consolidation was UDF-ward.

### 5. Muslim disenchantment with the CPM hardened into "enmity" — and consolidated against it

**Claim:** Open The Magazine's Kerala feature (drawing on historian A.M. Shinas, an unnamed IUML representative, scholar K. Ravi Raman and journalist C. Jamaat-e-Islami media (notably C. Dawood) painted the Left as indifferent to Muslim interests. The Federal cited senior CPI(M) leader T.M. Thomas Isaac and academic P.K. Pokker, who said the CPM's tonal drift had "blurred the line between Left politics and positions associated with Hindutva," unsettling core minority supporters.

### 7. Kerala Congress (Mani) wiped out — Pala fell, Christian Central-Travancore re-aligned, BJP's Christian outreach largely failed but BJP did damage KCM in Pala

**Claim:** Onmanorama's KCM autopsy reported Jose K. Mani lost Pala to UDF-backed independent Mani C. Kappen by 2,991 votes; KCM, the third-largest LDF partner, drew zero seats wherever it contested. P.J. Joseph's Kerala Congress (UDF) won seven seats, including direct contests against KCM in Changanassery, Kaduthuruthy and Thodupuzha. BJP's Shone George (P.C. George's son) surged in Pala from ≈10,000 votes (2021) to 35,304 votes — directly compressing Kappen's margin. Catholic World Report and ThePrint noted BJP's Christian candidates lost almost everywhere despite Modi's Trivandrum bishops meet — P.C. George, Shone George, Union MoS George Kurian and Anoop Antony all lost; "half a dozen other Christian candidates the BJP fielded in Christian pockets under its lotus symbol also lost" — but the BJP's three wins all came on Hindu candidates. The South First framed Central Travancore (Thrissur, Ernakulam, Kottayam, Idukki — 41 MLAs) as the "kingmaker" region the UDF reclaimed.

### 8. CMRL–Exalogic / Veena Vijayan SFIO chargesheet hardened anti-incumbency and personalised the corruption charge

**Claim:** Mathrubhumi, Onmanorama and Bar & Bench reported the SFIO chargesheet (April 2025) named CM Pinarayi Vijayan's daughter T. Veena and 26 others as accused under Section 447 of the Companies Act for ₹2.73 crore in payments from CMRL to Exalogic "for non-existent services," with the Income Tax Settlement Board flagging ₹1.72 crore. The South First framed this as how "Kerala CM's children became political lightning rods." The case became a recurring UDF talking point alongside the older Swapna Suresh / diplomatic-baggage gold smuggling case (in which Onmanorama reported a fresh January-2026 probe into smuggling of stolen Sabarimala-area valuables). Pinarayi's reduced Dharmadam margin (he trailed in six rounds, eventually winning ≈85,614 votes) was framed by Onmanorama as the visible scar of these scandals.

### 10. RSS / Hindu Aikya Vedi "Ekta Sammelanams" — temple-route Hindu mobilisation paid off in three southern Trivandrum/Kollam seats but not statewide

**Claim:** The News Minute and Newslaundry documented over 1,000 RSS-affiliated Ekta Sammelanams across Kerala between Feb–March 2026, often staged at temple premises with VHP and Hindu Aikya Vedi (state president R.V. Babu) participation, organised under the RSS centenary. Speeches included "Hindus are in danger" framing — claims that "Hindus cannot even sing bhajans freely" and the Sangh slogan "we are one." Onmanorama's BJP analysis and Open The Magazine attributed the BJP's three wins (Nemom — Rajeev Chandrasekhar by 4,165–4,978 votes over V. Sivankutty; Kazhakoottam — V. Rajendran) primarily to weak UDF candidates in three-cornered contests, plus border-district Hindutva receptivity (Palakkad/Trivandrum/Kasaragod) cited by scholar K. Ravi Raman. BJP statewide vote-share grew only ≈0.12%, suggesting deep-but-narrow consolidation rather than a wave.

### 13. K-Rail/SilverLine afterlife — Sreedharan's central alternative split UDF voices but the project's social cost stayed an LDF liability

**Claim:** The Federal reported the original SilverLine (529 km, 11 stations, ~1,300 hectares acquisition, ~₹63,000 crore) had become a flashpoint over land acquisition, wetland/paddy-field destruction, and debt. After the Centre refused permissions, E. Sreedharan (former BJP CM-face) advanced a Union-backed high-speed alternative, splitting Congress voices: K. Sudhakaran and Ramesh Chennithala opposed the original SilverLine; Leader of Opposition VD Satheesan was more nuanced. State Minister V. Abdurahiman publicly questioned Sreedharan's unilateral push. Although LDF tried to revive the project narrative, the residual memory of forced acquisition surveys remained a liability across the corridor districts the UDF eventually swept. (Note: K-Rail's electoral footprint is more diffuse than Munambam/Sabarimala; outlets named it among Pinarayi's "trifecta of liabilities" alongside gold smuggling and Exalogic.)
