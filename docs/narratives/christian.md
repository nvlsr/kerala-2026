## TL;DR

- **Each Christian sub-rite has its own historical alliance pattern.** Sub-rite identity is a second-order variable — religion share predicts UDF level better, but sub-rite tracks historical alignment and multi-cycle trajectory.
- **The 2026 swing was two stories on top of one another:**
  - **Story A — Restorations** (SM/Jacobite/Orthodox): returned to 2011-era UDF baselines after a 2-cycle LDF-leaning wobble. Continuity story.
  - **Story B — Flips** (Latin Catholic/Marthoma): structurally LDF-leaning 2011-2021, crossed parity in 2026 for the first time in our 4-cycle window. Shift story.
- **Latin Catholic was structurally LDF-leaning through 2011–2021** (just 4 of 28 UDF wins in BOTH 2016 and 2021). 2026 flipped 17 of those 28 ACs LDF→UDF. The biggest single sub-rite shift of 2026.
- **NDA hasn't gained in the Latin Catholic heartland.** Central Latin ACs (Ernakulam/Alappuzha/Kochi) — where Latin Catholics are most concentrated — show NDA share flat or shrinking. The Latin heartland is structurally NDA-resistant.
- **CSI cohort is geographically confounded.** All 5 CSI-cohort ACs are in Thiruvananthapuram district. The CSI "swing" is a Trivandrum-area phenomenon, not a denomination effect.
- **Three independent mitigation tests** confirm the cohort effect is community-driven, not artefact:
  - M1: Hindu→NDA absorption (partial, ~2pp magnitude in south Latin only)
  - M2: same-district control (Ernakulam Latin +16.0 vs SM +8.8 — a 7pp gap within one district)
  - M3: dose-response by Christian share (every Christian cohort shows ≥2pp HIGH−LOW gap; non-Christian shows none)
- **Christian-belt premium has been real since 2011 (~+3pp) and doubled to +6.4pp in 2026** — but is unevenly distributed across sub-rites.

## §2 — Geographic clusters

- **Latin Catholic**: coastal corridor — Trivandrum coast → Alleppey → Kochi → Kannur Latin coast → Kasaragod.
- **Syro-Malabar**: central Kerala interior — Kottayam, Idukki, Ernakulam interior, Thrissur interior, Mananthavady (Wayanad SM eparchy).
- **Indian Orthodox**: central Travancore — Tiruvalla / Niranam / Aranmula / Parumala corridor.
- **Jacobite Syrian**: 3 ACs in Ernakulam — Kunnathunad (Kitex/Twenty 20 home), Piravom (KC-Jacob), Kothamangalam.
- **Marthoma**: 2 ACs in Pathanamthitta — Aranmula and Ranni.
- **CSI**: 5 ACs all in Trivandrum district — Nedumangad, Nemom, Parassala, Kattakkada, Kovalam. **Geographically confounded with TVM-district patterns.**

The OSM-derived cohort layer (commit `42dee05`) is the first AC-level view of these clusters; before that, sub-rite geography was qualitatively known but not quantitatively mapped.

## §4 — NDA's Christian-cohort strategy (four prongs)

NDA mean share per Christian cohort (2021 → 2026):

| Cohort | NDA 2021 | NDA 2026 | Δ NDA | NDA wins 2026 |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Latin Catholic | 13.2 | 13.7 | **+0.5** | 1 (Kazhakkoottam) |
| Syro-Malabar | 11.8 | 14.7 | +2.9 | 0 |
| Indian Orthodox | 16.5 | 16.9 | +0.4 | 0 |
| Jacobite | 5.0 | 13.3 | **+8.3** | 0 (Twenty 20 effect) |
| Marthoma | 16.7 | 18.5 | +1.8 | 0 |
| **CSI** | **21.4** | **25.5** | **+4.2** | **1 (Nemom)** |
| Below threshold | 11.4 | 13.5 | +2.1 | 0 |

**Prong A — CSI/Trivandrum: direct.** All 5 CSI cohort ACs in TVM. NDA share 25.5% (highest of any Christian cohort), Nemom NDA win. Direct BJP candidate strategy in a geographic stronghold.

**Prong B — Jacobite Syrian: via Twenty 20.** Jacobite NDA share jumped +8.3pp (the biggest Christian-cohort NDA gain). Almost entirely the Twenty 20 effect: Kunnathunad alone delivered 25.1% T20 vote share. Twenty 20's Jacobite leadership (Sabu M. Jacob, Kitex) + Kunnathunad base do the work; pure BJP party share in Jacobite cohort is **0%**.

**Prong C — Syro-Malabar: dual approach.** Direct BJP candidates AND Twenty 20 spillover (Ernakulam-district SM ACs saw T20 7-19%). NDA share crept up +2.9pp — modest, no wins, but slightly above statewide baseline (+2.1).

**Prong D — Latin Catholic: no inroads.** NDA share went +0.5pp (basically flat). Despite being the largest Christian cohort, NDA gained no traction during a cycle that pulled Latin Catholics out of LDF and toward UDF — they went UDF, not NDA. The Latin heartland is structurally NDA-resistant.

## §8 — Two-stories synthesis

**Story A — Restorations** (SM, Jacobite, Orthodox)
- Started UDF-leaning in 2011 (SM 21 wins, Jacobite 3, Orthodox 5).
- LDF clawed close in 2016/2021.
- 2026 restored to ~2011 baselines. Continuity, not realignment.

**Story B — Flips** (Latin Catholic, Marthoma)
- Structurally LDF-leaning 2011–2021 (Latin 4/28 wins in 2016 AND 2021; Marthoma 0/2 both years).
- 2026 crossed parity for the first time in our 4-cycle window.
- 17 Latin ACs flipped LDF→UDF — biggest single sub-rite shift of 2026.

**Indian Orthodox is a third type** — didn't flip, didn't return, just stayed close, with UDF nudging slightly ahead (5/4/1).

**CSI is excluded** from the synthesis because cohort is geographically confounded with TVM district.

**Sub-rite identity is a historical-baseline variable.** The 2026 movement direction was statewide (anti-incumbency + Bishops' Council shifts in Catholic Kerala); what differed across cohorts is *which baseline they snapped back to or flipped away from*.

## §9 — Methodology: how we ruled out confounders

The cohort framework rests on AC-aggregate observations attributed to sub-rite communities. Three confounders bounded:

### M1 — Hindu-NDA absorption (south Latin)

> Alternative explanation: south Latin's smaller +6.8pp UDF swing reflects Hindu voters there shifting to NDA, masking what would otherwise be a uniform Christian shift.

Test: did NDA share GROW in south Latin between 2021→2026?

| Latin zone | n | Δ UDF | Δ LDF | **Δ NDA** |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| South (TVM + Kollam) | 11 | +6.8 | −8.1 | **+2.0** |
| Central (Alappuzha + Ernakulam + Thrissur) | 10 | +10.6 | −5.7 | −0.4 |
| North (Kannur + Kasaragod) | 7 | +10.2 | −8.7 | −0.5 |

**Partial support, small magnitude.** NDA grew +2pp in south Latin; shrank in central/north. About 2pp of the 3-4pp south-vs-rest UDF gap is consistent with Hindu→NDA absorption; rest is a genuinely smaller Christian swing.

Reading: south Latin shows BOTH a smaller Christian flip AND some Hindu→NDA absorption. The headline "Latin flipped" survives because even +6.8 reverses 4-of-28 UDF wins to 21-of-28.

### M2 — Same-district control (Ernakulam)

> Alternative explanation: cohort differences reflect district geographic clustering (SM = Kottayam-Idukki, Latin = coastal) rather than sub-rite identity.

Test: within Ernakulam district (contains both SM-cohort and Latin-cohort ACs), do they swing differently?

| Ernakulam cohort | n | UDF 2021 | UDF 2026 | Δ UDF | Δ NDA |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Syro-Malabar | 8 | 45.0 | 53.8 | +8.8 | +2.9 |
| **Latin Catholic** | **3** | **36.1** | **52.1** | **+16.0** | **+1.3** |
| Jacobite Syrian | 3 | 42.7 | 52.1 | +9.4 | +8.3 |

**Decisive.** Within Ernakulam (one district), Latin-cohort ACs swung +16.0pp UDF vs SM-cohort's +8.8pp — a **7pp gap** that district context cannot explain. Cohort identity survives same-district control.

Per-AC Ernakulam detail (sorted by ΔUDF):
- Kochi (Latin): UDF 31.5 → 48.2 (+16.7), ΔNDA +0.3
- Muvattupuzha (SM): UDF 44.6 → 61.0 (+16.4), ΔNDA +1.5
- Thrikkakara (SM): UDF 43.8 → 59.9 (+16.1), ΔNDA +4.1
- Ernakulam (Latin): UDF 41.7 → 57.5 (+15.8), ΔNDA +3.4
- Vypeen (Latin): UDF 35.0 → 50.5 (+15.5), ΔNDA 0.0
- Perumbavoor (SM): UDF 37.1 → 51.2 (+14.1), ΔNDA +4.8
- Kunnathunad (Jacobite): UDF 32.0 → 43.9 (+11.9), ΔNDA +20.5 (Twenty 20)
- Kothamangalam (Jacobite): UDF 42.2 → 53.1 (+10.9), ΔNDA +1.7
- Kalamassery (SM): UDF 39.7 → 49.7 (+10.1), ΔNDA +1.5
- Angamaly (SM): UDF 51.9 → 60.2 (+8.3), ΔNDA +1.3
- Piravom (Jacobite): UDF 53.8 → 59.2 (+5.4), ΔNDA +2.9
- Aluva (SM): UDF 49.0 → 53.2 (+4.2), ΔNDA +1.3
- Thripunithura (SM): UDF 42.1 → 45.8 (+3.7), ΔNDA +4.0
- Paravur (SM): UDF 51.9 → 49.6 (−2.3), ΔNDA +4.7

The cleanest reading: SM in Ernakulam started UDF-leaning (45%) and consolidated to 54% (+8.8pp). Latin in Ernakulam started LDF-leaning (36%) and flipped to 52% (+16pp). **Same district, two different shapes** — exactly the consolidation-vs-flip distinction.

### M3 — Within-cohort heterogeneity by Christian share

> Alternative explanation: cohort means mix ACs at 41% Christian (Vypeen) and 10% Christian (Trikaripur). If high- and low-share ACs swing differently, the cohort mean is mixing two distinct populations.

Test: split each cohort at its median Christian share. Do high-share and low-share halves swing differently?

| Cohort | n | LOW half (Christian%) | LOW Δ UDF | HIGH half (Christian%) | HIGH Δ UDF | Gap |
|---|---:|---|---:|---|---:|---:|
| Latin Catholic | 28 | 9.6 – 18.0 | +6.7 | 18.1 – 41.0 | **+11.3** | **+4.6pp** |
| Syro-Malabar | 31 | 14.2 – 33.9 | +5.4 | 35.5 – 64.5 | **+10.9** | **+5.4pp** |
| Indian Orthodox | 10 | 13.1 – 27.8 | +6.1 | 29.4 – 49.1 | +8.4 | +2.3pp |
| **Below-threshold control** | 61 | 0.2 – 2.0 | +7.2 | 2.0 – 16.1 | +5.5 | **−1.8pp** |

**Decisive dose-response.** Every Christian cohort shows a positive HIGH−LOW gap (~+2 to +5pp). The non-Christian below-threshold cohort shows the *opposite* (slightly negative).

**This is the predicted signature of a Christian-driven swing.** If the cohort swing were driven by Hindu/Muslim co-residents in cohort-labelled ACs, we'd expect *uniform* swings regardless of Christian share. Instead: more Christians in the AC → bigger UDF swing — only within Christian-cohort-labelled ACs.

**High-share subset claims (most defensible community-level inferences)**:
- High-share Latin (n=14, Christian 18-41%): +11.3pp UDF — Latin Catholic flipped strongly
- High-share SM (n=16, Christian 36-65%): +10.9pp UDF — SM consolidated strongly
- High-share Orthodox (n=5, Christian 29-49%): +8.4pp UDF — Orthodox edged toward UDF

These are the cleanest community-level claims in the dataset. The Sprint 2 cohort means (+9pp Latin, +8pp SM) were diluted by low-share ACs.

**South Latin's "smaller swing" reframed**: south Latin ACs are mostly low-Christian-share (mean ~17%), so the +6.8pp reading is the low-share statewide-baseline pattern, not a community-distinctive partial flip. High-Christian-share Latin everywhere swings +11pp+.

### Bonus — Hindu-NDA absorption is geographically separate

Thrissur district: below-threshold (Hindu-majority) ACs saw **NDA +10.8pp** between 2021–2026 (post-2024 Suresh Gopi / Hindu-belt BJP gain). But Thrissur SM-cohort ACs saw NDA share **shrink −0.9pp**.

**Hindu-NDA gain happens in Hindu-majority ACs, NOT Christian-cohort ACs.** The BJP Hindu-belt story and the Christian-cohort story are geographically separate.

For 2026 UDF vote share among Christian-cohort-labelled ACs (n=79):

| Predictor | R² |
|---|---:|
| Christian sub-rite cohort (one-way ANOVA) | 0.139 |
| Christian % of population (linear) | 0.169 |

For Δ UDF (2026 − 2021) swing:

| Predictor | R² |
|---|---:|
| Christian sub-rite cohort (one-way ANOVA) | **0.072** |

For Muslim cohort vs Muslim % (n=62, on UDF 2026):

| Predictor | R² |
|---|---:|
| Muslim sub-rite cohort (one-way ANOVA) | 0.014 |
| Muslim % of population (linear) | 0.237 |

**Reading**:
- Christian level: religion-share R² (0.169) slightly stronger than cohort R² (0.139) — share alone tells you most of the story.
- Christian swing: cohort R² (0.072) is ~2× religion-share R² (0.036) — **sub-rite distinction adds real info about how cohorts moved**, not where they started.

Caveat: one-way ANOVA inflates R² with many categories. Fair contest would apply df correction or use cross-validated R²; not done here. Point is **whether cohort term has explanatory power at all**, not which is "better".

## §11 — Mechanism, what's open, what would weaken

### What's directly observable

- 2026 cohort × win counts: Latin 21/6/1, SM 26/5/0, Orthodox 5/4/1, Jacobite 3/0/0, Marthoma 2/0/0, CSI 2/2/1.
- Multi-cycle UDF trajectories (per §3 tables) — pattern of restoration vs flip is robust.
- Within-Ernakulam Latin +16 vs SM +9 — cohort effect ≠ district effect.
- Dose-response by Christian share — cohort effect is Christian-community-driven.
- Hindu-NDA absorption is geographically separate from Christian-cohort story.

### What's inferred (defensible structural assumption)

- "Latin Catholic voters flipped from LDF to UDF in 2026" — community-level inference from AC-aggregate. Strongest in high-Christian-share Latin ACs (n=14, +11.3pp).
- "Syro-Malabar Catholic voters consolidated their UDF position" — high-share SM (n=16, +10.9pp).
- "Christian-cohort effect is community-driven" — supported by dose-response (M3), district-control (M2), Hindu-NDA-separation (bonus).

### What's NOT proven

- **Individual voter behaviour** — no exit polls or CSDS microdata. All community-level claims rest on AC-aggregate-to-community structural assumption.
- **Pre-2011 baseline** — historical data starts at 2011. "Structurally LDF-leaning Latin Catholic 2011-2021" is a 3-cycle observation, not 5-cycle. Pre-2011 patterns could differ.
- **The flip is durable.** A 2031 reversal would suggest 2026 was a one-off anti-incumbency wave channeled through Latin Catholic.
- **The Catholic sub-rite story is uniformly Syro-Malabar-driven within SM.** The SM cohort contains many sub-Catholic mixings (KC(M), KC-Jacob, KEC).

### What would weaken the headline

- **Pre-2011 baseline data** showing Latin Catholic was UDF-leaning in 2001/2006 → 2026 is a "longer return", not a flip; re-merges Stories A and B.
- **Individual-level data (exit polls / CSDS microdata)** showing AC-level swings don't map onto sub-rite-community swings.
- **2031 cycle reversal** where Latin Catholic swings back to LDF → suggests 2026 flip was cycle-specific.
- **Better Catholic-rite resolution** from sources beyond OSM POIs (diocesan boundaries, parish lists) → could refine SM vs Latin split where OSM has only generic "catholic" tags (~1,113 such POIs are assigned via district-prior).

## Methodology notes

### OSM POI → sub-rite cohort pipeline

1. **Raw OSM dump** (`scripts/fetch-osm-pow.ts`): pulls all places of worship in Kerala from Overpass API (~22k POIs).
2. **Classification** (`scripts/classify-osm-pow.ts`): normalizes religion + denomination tags, name-regex inference, Catholic disambiguation via district prior, spatial AC join.
3. **Aggregation** (`scripts/aggregate-ac-religion-pois.ts`): per-AC counts and dominant sub-rite.
4. **Voter-share metric**: `(sub-rite share among religion's POIs) × (religion population share, Census-2025)`. Cohort threshold = 5%; minimum classified-N = 3.

### Why the "Catholic"-generic disambiguation matters

OSM tagging often labels both Latin Catholic and Syro-Malabar parishes as just `denomination=catholic`. We disambiguate using a **district-level Catholic prior**:
- Trivandrum / Kollam / Alappuzha (coastal) → Latin Catholic
- Kottayam / Idukki / Ernakulam interior / Thrissur → Syro-Malabar
- Pathanamthitta → Syro-Malankara (small) + Malankara Orthodox

This handles ~1,113 generic-Catholic POIs. Where parish-name patterns are clear (e.g. "Marthoma", "Jacobite Syrian"), they override the district prior.

### Why the cohort isn't an exact voter-bloc

The cohort layer assumes within-religion POI distribution proxies for sub-rite population distribution. Reasonable for Catholic/Orthodox (large parishes); weaker for Pentecostal (small, possibly over-mapped). This assumption is in the falsifiers list above.

### Mitigation framework

Three tests bound the AC-aggregate → community ecological-fallacy concern:
- **M1** controls for Hindu vote-shift confounding (NDA Δ analysis)
- **M2** controls for district-level confounding (same-district SM vs Latin)
- **M3** controls for within-cohort religion-share heterogeneity (high vs low Christian share)

All three came back consistent with a Christian-community-driven cohort effect. The walkthrough cites them inline; this file has the full data tables.
