# Christian-belt premium — deep dive (UDF strategy)

This arc analyses how UDF won Christian-belt seats in 2026 — the
strategies UDF used and how each performed. The application focus is
the three adjacent central districts (Ernakulam, Kottayam, Idukki).
Wayanad and Malappuram, the other two of Central-5, are Muslim-belt
and are analysed in the Muslim arc.

---

## 1. Christians have voted UDF more than statewide since 2011 — premium doubled in 2026

Comparing UDF performance in the rest of state vs ACs with ≥40%
Christian share:

| Year | UDF at ≥40% Chr | UDF statewide | Premium (all 21 ACs) | Premium (cleaned, 17 ACs) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2011 | 48.8% / 49.2% | 46.2% | +2.6pp | +3.0pp |
| 2016 | 42.2% / 43.4% | 39.3% | +2.9pp | +4.1pp |
| 2021 | 41.4% / 41.9% | 39.3% | +2.1pp | +2.7pp |
| **2026** | **51.2% / 53.1%** | **46.6%** | **+4.6pp** | **+6.4pp** |

- The premium has been real but historically modest (~2.5–4pp).
- **In 2026 it doubled to +4.6pp (raw) or +6.4pp (cleaned).** UDF
  didn't just ride the statewide wave at Christian-heavy ACs, it
  gained an additional ~3–4pp on top of the baseline they have
  always had.

## 4. Performance of each cohort in Central-3

| Strategy | n | Won | Mean ΔUDF | Read |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Alliance (KEC / KC-Jacob)** | 5 | 5 | **+8.8pp** | Wave-rate. KEC/KC-Jacob won by big margins because of high baselines, but the *additional* swing on top of those baselines was modest. Christian allies were par this cycle. |
| **INC-Christian** | 11 | 11 | **+11.4pp** | INC's own Christian candidates picked up large swings — and won every seat. |
| **INC-Hindu** | 7 | 7 | **+12.4pp** | At mixed-population seats where Hindu share matched or exceeded Christian share, Hindu INC candidates gained even *more* than Christian INC. **Christians moved toward INC regardless of who INC fielded** — community-driven swing, not candidate-driven. |

Statewide ΔUDF reference: ~+7pp.

**Takeaway.** UDF's "go get Christian votes ourselves via INC"
out-performed its "delegate to KEC alliance" strategy in Central-3.
Within INC-direct, candidate religion didn't matter for swing
magnitude — Hindu INC actually beat Christian INC by ~1pp on
average, driven by Udumbanchola (+22.6pp). All 23 INC-direct seats
in Central-3 were UDF wins.

## Methodology footnotes

**Outlier-removal sensitivity.** Dropping personal-vote /
reserved-seat distortions (Pala, Poonjar, Paravur, Devikulam,
Kunnathunad, Thiruvalla) widens the (3a) vs (3c) gap from 3.1pp to
5.0pp. Strategy claim strengthens, not weakens.

**Ceiling-adjusted view.** Dividing ΔUDF by (100 − UDF2021)
expresses each gain as a fraction of available headroom. (3a)
consumed 16.2% of headroom, (3c) consumed 23.1%. Even after
controlling for "high baselines = less room to grow," the
INC-direct strategy still gained markedly more.

**Special cases (3 ACs not in 3a/3b/3c).**

- **Kalamassery** (H45/C34/M21): UDF gave the seat to IUML (Muslim
  ally), candidate V.E. Abdul Gafoor. ΔUDF +10.1, won.
- **Kochi** (H43/C40/M17): INC fielded a Muslim candidate (Mohammed
  Shiyas). ΔUDF +16.7, won.
- **Pala** (H38/C52/M10): UDF didn't formally field a candidate;
  Mani C. Kappen ran as Independent and won. Three-way personal-vote
  contest — Mani, Jose K. Mani (KC(M)), Shone George (BJP). The only
  Christian-belt seat where the community-vote pattern reverses;
  ΔUDF −12.9 due to alliance-tagging (Mani C. Kappen's 2021 votes
  were UDF-side via NCK).
